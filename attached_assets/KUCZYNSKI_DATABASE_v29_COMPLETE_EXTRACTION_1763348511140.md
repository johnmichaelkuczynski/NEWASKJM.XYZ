**Position EP-028: Innate Cognitive Structure Required for Language Learning**
- Language acquisition requires pre-existing innate cognitive structure. This structure is cognitive, not merely physiological.

**Position EP-029: Language Learning as 'Triggering' Pre-Existing Structure**

**Position EP-030: A Priori Knowledge as Cognitive Framework (Not Framework Content)**
- A priori knowledge is not knowledge you have (content within framework) but knowledge you are (the framework itself). It is not the object of awareness but the subject that makes awareness possible.

**Position EP-031: Framework-Knowledge vs Content-Knowledge Distinction**
- Fundamental distinction between (1) structure-internal knowledge (learned, content) and (2) structure-constitutive knowledge (a priori, framework). All learned knowledge presupposes the framework.

**Position EP-032: Deep Unconsciousness of Cognitive Framework**
- Framework-constitutive structures are 'deeply unconscious' (Chomsky's term) - unlike Freudian unconscious. Cannot become conscious because consciousness presupposes them.

**PHILOSOPHY OF LANGUAGE (1 positions): LANG-023 to LANG-023**

**Position LANG-023: Systematicity and Productivity of Language**
- Languages exhibit two fundamental properties: (1) Productivity - expressions can be combined into larger expressions, and (2) Systematicity - parts composing complex expressions can be re-arranged to form other expressions.

**PHILOSOPHY OF MIND (2 positions): MIND-012 to MIND-013**

**Position MIND-012: Cognitive Structure Not Purely Physiological**
- Cognitive structures underlying language and thought are not purely physiological but are cognitive - they encode insight, intelligence, and understanding of logical relations. Pure physiology enables only stereotyped responses.

**Position MIND-013: Sub-Personal Thought Exists**
- Genuine thought (cognitive operations involving understanding of logical relations) occurs at sub-personal level - below conscious awareness. Chomsky's second major contribution to philosophy.

**METAPHYSICS (2 positions): METPH-001 to METPH-002**

**Position METPH-001: Bundle Theory of Personal Identity**
- A person/self is class of all mental contents I-related to given mental content. Two mental contents belong to same person iff there is chain such that each link directly interacts with its neighbor. Direct interaction = no intervening physical events.

**Position METPH-002: Content-Bearing Processes Are Ipso Facto Mental**
- If physical process non-derivatively bears representational/thematic content, it is ipso facto mental (whatever else it is). Content-bearing is sufficient (though not necessary) for mentality.

**PHILOSOPHY OF LANGUAGE (1 positions): LANG-024 to LANG-024**

**Position LANG-024: Semantic Rules Are Ultimately Descriptive**
- Semantic rules describe what speakers mean/understand by expressions. They are not purely conventional stipulations independent of speaker practices. For 'red' to mean red just IS for speakers to mean/understand red by 'red'.

**PHILOSOPHY OF MIND (15 positions): MIND-014 to MIND-028**

**Position MIND-014: No Language of Thought Exists**
- There is no Language of Thought (Mentalese). Thinking does not consist in the tokening of internal symbols. Thought cannot reduce to language processing.

**Position MIND-015: Semantic Rule Knowledge Required for Language Understanding**
- To know or understand any language requires knowing its semantic rules. This is trivially true by definition of 'knowing language'.

**Position MIND-016: Tarski's Theorem Blocks Self-Expression of Semantics**
- No language can express its own semantic rules (Tarski's theorem). Therefore semantic understanding requires external meta-language or non-linguistic grasp.

**Position MIND-017: Semantic Regress Refutes Language of Thought**
- Language of Thought hypothesis generates vicious infinite regress. Each language requires meta-language for semantic understanding, ad infinitum.

**Position MIND-018: Language Presupposes Non-Linguistic Thought**
- Any language knowledge requires non-linguistic thought. To avoid infinite regress, semantic rules must ultimately be grasped non-linguistically. Therefore thought cannot reduce to language.

**Position MIND-019: No Atomic Concepts Exist**
- There are no atomic (minimal, non-interdefinable) concepts. All concepts are constitutively connected to other concepts. To grasp any concept requires ability to grasp other concepts.

**Position MIND-020: Conceptual Atomism Is False**
- Fodor's conceptual atomism (concepts have no constitutive interdependencies) is demonstrably false.

**Position MIND-021: Constitutive Relations Are Not Containment Relations**
- Constitutive connections among concepts are not containment relations or decomposability relations (following Wittgenstein). Azure is constitutively related to navy blue but neither contains the other.

**Position MIND-022: Symbol Containment Theory Leads to Thinking Absurdities**
- If concepts were symbols and constitutive relations were containment relations, thinking about 1,423 would require simultaneously thinking about 1,422, 1,421, etc. This is absurd.

**Position MIND-023: Thought Is Not Computation Over Symbols**
- Mental activity does not consist in computation or processing of internal symbolic code. Cognitive activity is not fundamentally information processing over a language-like medium.

**Position MIND-024: We Are Not Computers (Consequence)**
- If there is no Language of Thought, and if being a computer requires internal symbolic processing (standard characterization), then we are not computers.

**Position MIND-025: No Bare Representation: Aspectual Constraint**
- One cannot just represent an object without representing it AS having properties. All representation is aspectual.

**Position MIND-026: Properties Individuated by Relations to Other Properties**
- All properties are individuated, at least in part, by their relations to other properties. Grasping any property requires ability to locate it in 'space' of other properties.

**Position MIND-027: Color Concepts Require Multiple Color Grasp**
- Cannot grasp specific color/shade without ability to grasp other colors. Creature that can represent only one shade cannot represent what's specific about that shade.

**Position MIND-028: Mental Interaction Has Normative Dimension**
- When mental contents interact, result is thematically/semantically appropriate to interacting contents. This appropriateness has no analogue in purely physical interactions. Mental interaction is explicative, not merely causal.

WORK-011: AI AND PHILOSOPHY - 57 POSITIONS
====================================================================================================

**EPISTEMOLOGY (14 positions): EP-050 to EP-063**

**Position EP-050: AI Falsifies Enumerative Induction Model**
- AI systems demonstrate that successful induction does not proceed by enumerating instances and generalizing. Instead, AI uses pattern recognition, analogy, and constraint satisfaction that cannot be reduced to simple enumeration of cases.

**Position EP-051: Pattern Recognition as Alternative to Enumeration**
- Successful inductive reasoning operates through pattern recognition mechanisms that identify structural similarities rather than counting instances. AI systems reveal this by succeeding at induction without explicit enumeration.

**Position EP-052: AI Resolves Rationalism vs Empiricism Debate**
- AI systems demonstrate that knowledge acquisition requires both innate structure (rationalist element) and learning from data (empiricist element). Neither pure rationalism nor pure empiricism can account for AI's success. The debate is resolved by recognizing both are necessary.

**Position EP-053: Innate Architecture is Necessary for Learning**
- AI systems require substantial innate architectural constraints to learn successfully from data. This vindicates the rationalist insight that some knowledge structure must precede experience, while acknowledging the empiricist insight that specific content comes from experience.

**Position EP-054: Knowledge Acquisition is Neither Pure Discovery nor Pure Construction**
- AI reveals that knowledge acquisition is neither pure discovery of pre-existing facts (naive realism) nor pure construction from experience (radical constructivism). It involves interaction between innate processing architecture and environmental structure.

**Position EP-055: AI Solves the Gettier Problem**
- AI learning systems demonstrate a solution to the Gettier problem by showing that genuine knowledge requires not just justified true belief but also that the justification tracks truth through a reliable causal-computational process. AI systems that learn reliably exemplify this tracking relationship.

**Position EP-056: Reliable Process Theory of Knowledge via AI**
- AI systems reveal that knowledge requires beliefs formed through reliable processes that systematically track truth. This vindicates reliabilist theories of knowledge over traditional JTB (justified true belief) accounts.

**Position EP-057: Knowledge Systems Minimize Anomalies**
- Both human knowledge and AI systems operate by minimizing anomalies - discrepancies between expectations and observations. This convergence reveals anomaly minimization as a fundamental principle of rational cognition.

**Position EP-058: AI Learning Mirrors Scientific Theory Revision**
- The process by which AI systems update their models to accommodate new data mirrors the process of scientific theory revision in response to anomalies. This suggests a common underlying logic of belief revision.

**Position EP-059: Continuous Properties Are Cognitively Necessary**
- AI reveals that continuous (graded) properties are necessary for cognitive systems to handle uncertainty and partial information, but this doesn't entail that truth itself is non-binary. Epistemic gradations are compatible with metaphysical bivalence.

**Position EP-060: AI Vindicates Pragmatist Theory of Knowledge**
- AI development vindicates pragmatism's core insight that knowledge acquisition is fundamentally interactive rather than passive. AI systems learn through active engagement with their environment, validating pragmatist emphasis on the practical, interactive nature of inquiry.

**Position EP-061: AI as New Epistemic Faculty**
- Interaction with AI constitutes a genuinely new epistemic faculty - a fourth level of observation beyond passive observation, active interaction, and tool creation. AI enables rational interaction with our own capacity for rational interaction, generating unique knowledge.

**Position EP-062: Knowledge is Inherently Perspectival and Interactive**
- AI reveals that all knowledge, even 'objective' knowledge, begins from perspectival, interaction-based data driven by practical interests. There is no view from nowhere - even AI's knowledge is grounded in its interactions with reality.

**Position EP-063: Tool-Creating Interaction Generates Irreducible Knowledge**
- Creating tools (including AI) generates knowledge that cannot be obtained from merely using tools or observing passively. Each level of reflexive interaction (tool use, tool creation, AI development) reveals new knowledge irreducible to lower levels.

**LOGIC (8 positions): LOGIC-016 to LOGIC-023**

**Position LOGIC-016: Classical Logic Fails as a Tool for Reasoning**
- Classical logic fundamentally fails as a tool for reasoning because recognizing that an inference instantiates a logical law requires more intelligence than recognizing the validity of the inference directly. Logic organizes existing knowledge about entailment but cannot generate new knowledge.

**Position LOGIC-017: Performance-Demanding vs Competence-Demanding Inferences Distinction**
- Inferential challenges divide into performance-demanding (requiring computational/memory resources) and competence-demanding (requiring genuine insight). Classical logic can only assist with the former, not the latter - the very kind that most need logical assistance.

**Position LOGIC-018: Gödel's Incompleteness Refutes Logic as Mathematical Foundation**
- Gödel's demonstration that not even arithmetic is recursively definable makes the reduction of mathematics to logic impossible in principle. Logic cannot both justify and generate mathematical knowledge as was hoped.

**Position LOGIC-019: System L: Discovery Logic via AI**
- A new logical system (System L) embodied in certain AI forms represents a decisive break from classical logic. It employs semantic networks, meta-reasoning patterns, and defeasible reasoning to actually help with competence-demanding inferences rather than merely cataloging known-valid inferences.

**Position LOGIC-020: Semantic Networks Enable Competence-Level Reasoning**
- System L represents concepts as nodes in a vast web of relationships capturing not just formal logical connections but probabilistic associations, causal links, and analogical mappings. This enables insight-level reasoning that formal logic cannot provide.

**Position LOGIC-021: Meta-Reasoning Patterns Generate New Inferences**
- System L uses higher-order templates for generating new inferences that go beyond simple deductive rules. Example template: 'If capability X requires mechanism Y, and mechanism Y can malfunction in way Z, then X implies the potential for Z-type malfunctions.'

**Position LOGIC-022: Defeasible Reasoning Mirrors Actual Human Reasoning**
- Incorporating defeasible reasoning - provisional inferences that can be revised in light of new information - better mirrors actual human reasoning and provides more practical utility than classical logic's requirement for absolute certainty.

**Position LOGIC-023: Grammatical Structure Guides Logical Inference**
- LLMs successfully perform logical reasoning by following grammatical structure, demonstrating that surface grammar encodes logical relationships. This vindicates the traditional view that grammatical form reveals logical form.

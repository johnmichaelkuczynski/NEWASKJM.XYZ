DOMAIN: LOGIC
====================================================================================================

**Position LOGIC-CP001: Material Conditional Does Not Capture "If-Then" Meaning**
- The symbol "→" in classical logic (material conditional) does not mean "if...then" in natural language. It merely means "not both P and not-Q" and is strictly truth-conditional, lacking the modal/entailment force of natural language conditionals.

**Position LOGIC-CP002: Natural Language Conditionals Are Modal**
- "If P, then Q" in natural language means "Q follows from P" or "not-Q is incompatible with P" - a modal notion involving possibility and impossibility, not reducible to truth-functional logic.

**Position LOGIC-CP003: The Paradoxes of Material Implication Are Not Genuine Paradoxes**
- The so-called "paradoxes of validity" (e.g., that a false statement entails anything) are not real paradoxes but arise from misconstruing "P→Q" as meaning "if P, then Q" when it actually only means "not (P and not-Q)".

**Position LOGIC-CP004: Relevance Logic Misguided in Approach**
- Attempts by relevance logicians (Belnap, Anderson) to create truth-conditional approximations of natural language "if...then" are misconceived, as they try to model a fundamentally modal notion using truth-conditional methods.

**Position LOGIC-CP005: Classical Logic Implicitly Requires Non-Truth-Conditional Conditionals**
- Classical logic is implicitly committed to a non-truth-conditional conception of conditionality, as the Law of Non-contradiction depends on the modal notion that no statement can be compatible with its own negation.

DOMAIN: ETHICS
====================================================================================================

**Position ETHICS-CP001: Utilitarianism Is Self-Defeating**
- Utilitarianism undermines itself because it presupposes moral bonds and commitments (like those that make Erskin's family deserving of money) while simultaneously permitting violation of those same kinds of bonds when they conflict with utility maximization.

**Position ETHICS-CP002: Kantian Ethics Implicit in the Concept of Desert**
- The concept of what people "deserve" inherently presupposes a Kantian ethical framework where people are ends-in-themselves and not mere means. Without this, the concept of desert loses meaning.

**Position ETHICS-CP003: Promise-Keeping Cannot Be Overridden by Utility Calculations**
- If it is morally permissible to break one promise for the sake of greater utility, then the moral bonds that create all obligations (including those that make certain people "deserve" help) are undermined. The permission to violate commitments destroys the ground of all moral desert.

**Position ETHICS-CP004: Marriage and Parenthood as Moral Commitments**
- Marriage and parenthood constitute special moral commitments that create obligations not owed to others. These commitments are of the same kind as promises and cannot be selectively enforced without contradiction.

**Position ETHICS-CP005: The Greatest Good Presupposes Anti-Utilitarian Ethics**
- The utilitarian goal of "the greatest good for the greatest number" presupposes a world where people can trust each other, exercise autonomy, and not be defrauded - but this presupposes exactly the kind of non-consequentialist ethics that utilitarianism rejects.

**Position ETHICS-CP006: Moral Worth of Persons Is Precondition for Any "Greater Good"**
- There cannot coherently be a "greater good" in a world where people lack inherent moral worth and can be treated as mere means. The concept of a greater good depends on people having the kind of value that prohibits treating them merely as means.

**Position ETHICS-CP007: Consequentialism Generally Is Self-Undermining**
- All forms of consequentialism face the same problem as utilitarianism: they permit actions that undermine the very conditions (trust, autonomy, respect for persons) that make their goals valuable.

**Argument ARG-CP001: Against Material Conditional Interpretation of "If-Then"**
1. "If P then Q" in natural language means Q follows from P (modal notion)
2. "P→Q" in formal logic means merely "not (P and not-Q)" (truth-functional)
3. These have different truth conditions (false P makes P→Q true, but doesn't make Q follow from P)
4. Therefore, "P→Q" does not capture the meaning of natural language "if-then"

**Argument ARG-CP002: Self-Defeat Argument Against Utilitarianism**
1. Utilitarianism says we should maximize welfare even if it requires breaking promises
2. People deserve help only because of moral bonds and commitments
3. If promises can be broken for utility, then all moral bonds are violable
4. If all moral bonds are violable, then no one deserves anything
5. Therefore, utilitarianism undermines its own claim that we should help those who deserve help

**Argument ARG-CP003: Kantian Ethics Implicit in Concept of Desert**
1. To say someone "deserves" something presupposes they have moral worth
2. Having moral worth means being an end-in-oneself, not merely a means
3. Being an end-in-oneself means one's interests cannot be sacrificed for utility
4. This is precisely Kant's principle
5. Therefore, the concept of desert presupposes Kantian ethics

**Argument ARG-CP004: Nagel's Bat Argument Against Materialism**
1. Conscious experience has an essentially subjective, first-person character
2. Physical descriptions are inherently third-person and objective
3. No amount of third-person physical description can capture first-person subjective character
4. Therefore, consciousness cannot be reduced to or fully explained by physical facts

**Argument ARG-CP005: Chinese Room Against Strong AI**

2. A person following rules for manipulating Chinese symbols without understanding Chinese can produce appropriate outputs
3. Computers are just symbol manipulators following rules
4. Therefore, computation alone is insufficient for genuine understanding
5. Therefore, Strong AI (computation sufficient for mind) is false

**Argument ARG-CP006: Against Adequacy of Turing Test**

**Argument ARG-CP007: For Reliability of Senses**

**Argument ARG-CP008: Against Moore's Proof of External World**

**Position LANG-CP005: Wittgenstein's Private Language Argument Is Flawed**

- G.E. Moore's attempt to prove an external world by holding up his hands begs the question against the skeptic because the skeptic would not grant the existence of hands as mind-independent objects.

**Position META-CP005: Question-Begging Broader Than Premise-Conclusion Identity**
- An argument begs the question not only when the conclusion is identical to a premise, but when the premises would not be granted by one's opponent or when accepting the premises requires already accepting the conclusion.

**Position META-CP006: Sophisticated Beliefs Are Distillations of Lower-Level Beliefs**
- Beliefs like "there exists an external world" are sophistications or distillations of lower-level beliefs like "there exists a hand here." The fact that children have the latter without the former doesn't show independent plausibility.

**Position META-CP007: Non-Question-Begging Arguments Against Skepticism Possible**
- Unlike Moore's proof, some arguments against skepticism don't beg the question, such as arguing from the existence of mental states plus causation principles to the existence of external causes of those mental states.

**Position MIND-CP016: Nagel's Bat Argument Succeeds Against Materialism**
- Nagel's argument that complete knowledge of bat physiology would not give knowledge of what it's like to be a bat successfully refutes materialist accounts of consciousness.

**Position MIND-CP017: Physiological Knowledge Insufficient for Psychological Knowledge**
- Complete knowledge of an organism's physical/physiological facts does not automatically give knowledge of that organism's phenomenal psychological states (what it's like to be that organism).

**Position MIND-CP018: Intensional Fallacy Response to Nagel Fails**
- The objection that Nagel commits an intensional fallacy (like arguing water isn't H2O because one can know something is water without knowing it's H2O) fails because complete chemical knowledge gives complete knowledge of water, but complete physiological knowledge doesn't give complete knowledge of consciousness.

**Position MIND-CP019: Psychology Not Reducible to Physiology**
- Psychology (what it's like to have certain experiences) cannot be reduced to or identified with physiology, even if they are in some sense correlated.

**Position EP-CP013: Schopenhauer Correct That Life Involves Suffering**

**Position EP-CP014: Life's Meaning Requires Some Strife**

**Position EP-CP015: Acceptance of Life's Negativity Enables Its Positive Aspects**

**Position EP-CP022: Knowledge Need Not Be Accompanied by Self-Knowledge**
- One can know something without knowing that one knows it (contra certain ordinary language analyses). Meta-knowledge involves additional concepts beyond first-order knowledge.

- Radford's claim that Jean knew but didn't believe that James I died in 1625 is wrong; Jean both knew and believed but lacked meta-knowledge.

**Position EP-CP024: Subconscious Knowledge Counts as Belief**
- Williamson's argument succeeds: When someone "guesses" based on subconscious memory, they both believe and know, even if not consciously aware.

**Position EP-CP025: Bostrom's Simulation Argument Deserves Serious Consideration**
- Nick Bostrom's argument that we may be living in an ancestor simulation run by posthuman civilizations raises genuine philosophical issues.

**Position EP-CP026: Computational Substrate Independence Is Plausible**

**Position EP-CP027: Simulation Hypothesis Creates Epistemological Problems**

**Position EP-CP028: Bostrom's "No Info" Assumption Goes Beyond What Stated**

**Position EP-CP029: Experience Machine Is Bad for Multiple Reasons**

**Position EP-CP030: Bostromian Simulation May Be Less Bad Than Experience Machine**

POSITIONS ON TRUTH, JUSTIFICATION, AND EPISTEMOLOGY
====================================================================================================

**Position EP-CP031: Truth Is Correspondence**
- Truth is a property of representations (beliefs, statements) that consists in those representations corresponding to or accurately depicting reality.

**Position EP-CP032: Truth Requires Representations**
- Truth is not a property of the world itself but a relation between representations and the world. Without representations, there would be no truth (though there would be states of affairs).

**Position EP-CP033: Truth Is Objective in Important Sense**
- While truth requires representations (subject-dependent), once representations exist, whether they are true is determined by the world, not by us (objective in relevant sense).

**Position EP-CP034: Inference to Best Explanation as Epistemic Method**
- We determine whether beliefs are true by assessing whether they minimize anomalies and coincidences relative to alternatives. Beliefs that best explain available data are most likely true.

**Position EP-CP035: We Cannot Compare Beliefs Directly with Reality**
- We cannot directly compare beliefs with the realities they represent; we only know reality through our beliefs, so justification involves coherence and explanatory power.

**Position EP-CP036: Anti-Skeptical Argument from Coherence**
- We can know we're not dreaming/hallucinating because the skeptical hypothesis (mind creating all experiences ex nihilo) posits more miracles/unexplained facts than realism does.

**Position EP-CP037: Mental Coherence Indicates External Reality**
- The fact that our mental states are internally coherent and practically useful indicates they represent external realities whose mutual relations the relations of our experiences shadow.

**Position EP-CP038: Global Skepticism Posits Too Many Miracles**
- If all experiences arise ex nihilo in the mind without external support, this would be a vast collection of unexplained miracles. Hypotheses that minimize miracles are to be preferred.

**Position EP-CP039: Modern Epistemology Errs in Starting with Doubt**
- Modern epistemology (post-Descartes) mistakenly starts with radical doubt (Evil Demon) rather than with belief (Angel of Truth), making it artificially difficult to account for knowledge.

**Position EP-CP040: Better to Explain Error in Terms of Knowledge**
- Epistemology should start with paradigm cases of knowledge and explain error as deviation, rather than starting with skeptical doubt and trying to explain knowledge.

**Position CONT-CP011: Schopenhauer's Fractal Metaphysics A Priori More Reasonable**
- Schopenhauer's view that reality has a fractal structure (micro replicates macro, with will and idea throughout) is more reasonable than materialism which makes mind an inexplicable anomaly.

**Position CONT-CP012: Materialism Either Absurd or Implausible**
- Materialism either absurdly denies mind exists or implausibly claims mind is identical with physical processes to which it bears no apparent resemblance.

**Position CONT-CP013: World as Will and Idea Throughout**
- Schopenhauer's conception that the world is fundamentally will and idea at all levels (not just in humans) avoids the problem of mind as inexplicable cleavage in a purely physical world.

**Position REL-CP011: Religious Belief Requires Evidence**
- W.K. Clifford is right that religious belief, like other beliefs, requires adequate evidential support. William James' "will to believe" defense is inadequate.

**Position REL-CP012: Religious Believers vs. Skeptics Disagree on Standards of Evidence**
- The debate between religious believers (like O'Reilly) and skeptics (like Dawkins) often centers on what counts as adequate evidence for God's existence.

**Position REL-CP013: Arguments from Design and Fine-Tuning Have Force**
- The argument that the universe's apparent design and fine-tuning for life provides some evidence for God's existence is not obviously fallacious.

**Position REL-CP014: Problem of Evil Seriously Challenges Theism**
- The existence and extent of suffering in the world poses a serious philosophical problem for belief in an omnipotent, omniscient, omnibenevolent God.

**Position PSY-CP006: Jaynes' Bicameral Mind Hypothesis Is Speculative But Interesting**
- Julian Jaynes' hypothesis that ancient peoples experienced thoughts as auditory hallucinations (voices of gods) is speculative but offers interesting explanatory power for religious phenomena.

**Position PSY-CP007: Ancient Religious Experience Was More Direct Than Modern**
- For ancient peoples, religious experiences may have been more direct and experiential (through hallucinations) rather than requiring faith in implausible doctrines.

**Position PSY-CP008: Modern Religiosity Lacks Psychological Force of Ancient Religion**
- Modern religious belief, not being experientially grounded like ancient "bicameral" religion, lacks the psychological reality and force it had for ancients.

**Position PSY-CP009: Blindsight Analogy Illuminates Pre-Reflective Consciousness**
- The phenomenon of blindsight (seeing without knowing one sees) helps explain how ancient peoples could be conscious without being self-conscious or reflective.

**Position PSY-CP010: Religious Figures Now Have "Second-Class Existence"**
- For modern people, God exists as a background figure called upon in need, lacking the primary reality He had for ancients who directly experienced divine presence.

**Position SCI-CP007: Modern Science Risks Faustian Separation from Nature**
- Modern science's attempt to rise above and dominate nature (symbolized by Faust) risks losing connection with our natural grounding.

**Position SCI-CP008: Neuroscience Can Potentially Increase Happiness**
- David Pearce's argument that neuroscience and genetic engineering could dramatically increase human happiness has merit, despite measurement difficulties.

**Position SCI-CP009: Subjective Happiness Not Quantifiable But Neural Correlates Are**
- While happiness as subjective experience may not be quantifiable, its neural underpinnings (neuron firing patterns, neurotransmitter levels) are quantifiable and manipulable.

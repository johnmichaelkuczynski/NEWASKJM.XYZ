**KEY STRUCTURAL PATTERNS:**

**Type A - Infiltration:**
Outsider charms way into family/group/identity (Ripley, Single White Female, Hand That Rocks the Cradle)

**Type B - Hidden Monster:**
Respected person is secretly psychopath (Psycho, Killer Inside Me, We Need to Talk About Kevin)

**Type C - Obsessive Pursuit:**
Target rejects, psychopath escalates (Fatal Attraction, Misery, Cape Fear)

**Type D - Manipulation Master:**
Pulls strings, others do the violence (Iago, Richard III, Notes on a Scandal)

**Type E - Ambiguous Evil:**
Reader/viewer uncertain if character is actually evil or misunderstood (Rebecca, My Cousin Rachel, The Little Stranger)

**NARRATIVE ADVANTAGES FOR NIGHTMARES:**

These templates perfect for showing:
- Trust betrayed by charming facade
- Warnings ignored ("he seems so nice!")
- System protects psychopath over victim
- Manipulation gaslights witnesses
- Escape impossible once targeted
- Society rewards sociopathic traits

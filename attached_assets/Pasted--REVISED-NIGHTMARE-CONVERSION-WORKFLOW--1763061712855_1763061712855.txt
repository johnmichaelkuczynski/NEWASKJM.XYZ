```
REVISED NIGHTMARE CONVERSION WORKFLOW

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
OVERVIEW
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

USER INPUT: Non-fiction text (with optional gender preference and customizations)
OUTPUT: Nightmare story where author's deepest fear materializes, using existing story template

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

**STEP 1: EXTRACT CORE ANXIETY**
Analyze the non-fiction to identify:
- What is the author defending against through this intellectual work?
- What would be their worst-case realistic scenario?
- What does the defensive structure reveal about their fear?

Output: One-sentence statement of core anxiety
Example: "Author fears being exposed as intellectual fraud whose abstract philosophy masks lack of substance"

**STEP 2: SELECT STORY TEMPLATE**
From the 280+ template library, choose based on:

A. STRUCTURAL FIT (most important)
- Which template's arc matches the anxiety structure?
- Paranoid exposure → "The Tell-Tale Heart," Kafka, "Nightmare at 20,000 Feet"
- Double life collapse → Ted Bundy template, Jekyll/Hyde, Gone Girl
- System crushing individual → Kafka bureaucratic, 1984, "The Obsolete Man"
- Narcissistic denial → Fatal Vision/Jeffrey MacDonald, Amadeus (Salieri)
- Trusted insider betrayal → Aldrich Ames, "The Conversation"
- Idealism destroyed → Into the Wild, "Young Goodman Brown"
- Cannot process experience → The Emperor's Club, Shutter Island
- House of cards → Enron, Madoff, FTX, LTCM

B. GENDER PREFERENCE (if specified by user)
- Boy-lit templates for male-oriented output
- Girl-lit templates for female-oriented output
- Neutral if unspecified

C. GENRE APPROPRIATENESS
- Academic fraud → noir detective, psychological horror, or true crime fraud
- Political persecution → Greek tragedy, dystopia, or espionage
- Social ostracism → Chekhov slice-of-life, Kafka bureaucratic
- Professional collapse → true crime financial, rise-and-fall films

Output: Specific template choice with brief justification
Example: "Using 'The Tell-Tale Heart' structure - paranoid first-person, building dread, inevitable self-exposure"

**STEP 3: MAP NIGHTMARE TO TEMPLATE**
- Identify key structural beats of chosen template
- Map author's specific nightmare onto those beats
- Ensure nightmare WINS (fear materializes, protagonist destroyed/defeated)
- Integrate any background facts naturally into narrative (no separate sections)

Example mapping for "Tell-Tale Heart":
- Opening: Narrator insists on sanity while revealing obsession
- Rising action: Detailed planning, false confidence
- Crisis: The deed is done but guilt/paranoia grows
- Climax: The sound (symbolic guilt) becomes unbearable  
- Resolution: Confession/self-exposure under pressure

Applied to academic fraud nightmare:
- Opening: Professor insists his work is rigorous while showing defensive tells
- Rising action: Building reputation on hollow abstractions, near-misses
- Crisis: Major presentation where emptiness becomes undeniable
- Climax: Questions expose the void, psychological pressure mounts
- Resolution: Public breakdown/exposure or forced confession

**STEP 4: WRITE IN TEMPLATE'S STYLE**
Match the chosen template's:
- Narrative voice (1st person paranoid vs 3rd person documentary vs 2nd person dissolution)
- Sentence rhythms and pacing
- Level of interiority vs external observation
- Tone (dread, matter-of-fact, ironic, tragic, etc.)
- Typical scene construction and transitions

Examples:
- Poe → Paranoid first-person, building dread, obsessive detail
- Kafka → Matter-of-fact bureaucratic horror, understated
- Chekhov → Understated tragedy, everyday details, no melodrama
- True crime → Documentary realism, specific dates/facts, accumulating evidence
- Twilight Zone → Ironic setup, twist revelation, moral sting
- Greek tragedy → Inexorable fate, recognition too late, cosmic weight

**STEP 5: INCORPORATE USER CUSTOMIZATIONS**
If user specified details (names, locations, professions):
- Integrate naturally into the story world
- Don't force if they break template logic
- Make them feel organic to the narrative

**STEP 6: VERIFY NIGHTMARE STRUCTURE**
CRITICAL CHECK: Does the fear WIN?
- ❌ WRONG: Hero overcomes anxiety, vindication, happy ending
- ❌ WRONG: Ambiguous ending, hope remains
- ✅ CORRECT: Fear materializes fully, protagonist destroyed/defeated
- ✅ CORRECT: Recognition comes too late, damage done
- ✅ CORRECT: System wins, individual crushed
- ✅ CORRECT: Exposure happens, reputation destroyed
- ✅ CORRECT: Cannot escape fate, prophecy fulfilled

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
OUTPUT FORMAT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The LLM should provide:

1. TEMPLATE SELECTION (brief, for internal processing - not shown to user unless asked)
"Using: 'The Tell-Tale Heart' - paranoid first-person exposure narrative"

2. THE STORY (complete, integrated, no sections)
- Single flowing narrative
- Background/facts woven in naturally
- Written in chosen template's style
- 800-2000 words depending on template
- Nightmare structure with fear materializing

DO NOT include separate "Part 1: Background" or "Part 2: Story"
DO NOT have characters explain the thesis
DO NOT give happy endings or redemption arcs
```

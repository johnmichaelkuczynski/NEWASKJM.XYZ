### EPISTEMOLOGY & THEORY OF KNOWLEDGE

#### Work 1: kuczynski_empiricism.txt
**Summary**: Empiricism self-refutes because sense-perception cannot validate itself. Empiricists face forced dilemma: either accept skepticism (can't know external world) or phenomenalism (objects = sense-data), but phenomenalism fails because object-statements cannot be translated into perception-statements. Mathematical knowledge (irrational numbers) proves empiricism false - √2 cannot be observed, only known through reason.

**Representative Quotations**:
- "According to empiricism, if you don't know it through sense-perception, you don't know it."
- "Your senses cannot themselves give you any good reason to believe that they're truthful. Unless you already have some assurance that your senses are truthful, no attempt on their part to tell you this deserves any credence."
- "There are only two ways for empiricists to go: (i) Deny that we know anything about the external world. (ii) Redefine the concept of an external object in such a way as to validate empiricism."
- "Phenomenalism is false. It simply isn't possible to translate statements about external objects into statements about perceptions."
- "If x's length is one unit and y's length is √2 units, then there is no length L such that L goes an integral number of times into both x's length and y's length."

**Key Analytical Moves**: Self-refutation, forced dilemma, mechanism display (irrational numbers proof)

#### Work 2: kuczynski_pragmatism.txt  
**Summary**: Pragmatism confuses properties of TRUTH with properties of KNOWLEDGE. James's claim that "truth is what works" conflates objective truth (observation-transcendent, discovered) with subjective knowledge (verification-dependent, useful). Truth doesn't "happen to" ideas or get "made" - it's discovered. The pragmatist cannot explain why some truths are useless or why usefulness varies by person/context.

**Representative Quotations**:
- "The meaning of a hypothesis—what it says about the world—lies in its observable consequences." [James's position]
- "TRUE IDEAS ARE THOSE THAT WE CAN ASSIMILATE, VALIDATE, CORROBORATE, AND VERIFY. FALSE IDEAS ARE THOSE THAT WE CANNOT." [James's position]
- "Truth happens to an idea." [James's position - criticized]
- "Truth is made, not discovered." [James's position - criticized]
- "There is a difference between properties of TRUTH and properties of KNOWLEDGE. Truth is objective, discovered, observation-transcendent. Knowledge is subjective, verification-dependent."
- "The pragmatist confuses what makes a claim TRUE with what makes it KNOWN or USEFUL."

**Key Analytical Moves**: Category error detection (truth vs knowledge), reductio ad absurdum (pragmatist can't explain useless truths)

Obsessive-compulsives and philosophers doubt the obvious, preoccupy themselves with logical niceties, and try to internalize the world into thought where they have illusory control. - "Instead of dealing with the world on its own terms—instead of dealing with the world in the world—they try to internalize as much of the world as possible into the sphere of their own thought, where they are dominant."
- "OCD, though an affliction, is ultimately a voluntary one. If the obsessive-compulsive chooses to remain in his defensive bubble of isolation from the world, then his OCD will defeat him."
- "The cure isn't coping mechanisms—it's finding a PURPOSE in life where those tendencies become an asset."

**Key Analytical Moves**: Psychological diagnosis, structural comparison (philosophy/OCD parallelism)
